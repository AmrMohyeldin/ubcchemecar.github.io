Introduction

UBC Chem-E-Car is one of 22 undergraduate teams recognized as an Engineering Design Team in the University of British Columbia.

We specialize in safely transforming potential energy stored in chemicals to mechanical energy as well as accurate control of chemical reactions for use as chemical timing.

The UBC Chem-E-Car team is one of the few EDCs that represent chemical engineers. We provide a platform for gaining hands-on experience in clean energy production. Many of our former team members have started exciting careers fuel cell and battery-related work

We compete in the annual American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE) Chem-E-Car competition.

Shoe-box sized car

The competition involves making a shoe-box sized car that travels a certain distance with a given cargo in under 2 minutes.

Stopping Mechanism

To complicate the otherwise trivial task, the car’s stopping mechanism must be chemical – no physical or electronic clocks and sensors can be used for stopping.

The Challenge

Furthermore, we are only given the cargo and travel distance 1 hour ahead of the competition. Extensive knowledge of the reactions involved are required for accurate control of the car.

What We Do

Want to join us? We will not turn away any dedicated, hard working students that contribute to the project.

Chemical

Design of car stopping mechanism using the iodine clock reaction. Involves wet lab experiments

Battery

Research battery material and composition, design battery casing, perform circuitry integration

Electrical

Circuit board fabrication, circuitry layout design, soldering and assemblying PCB and Arduino programming

Mechanical

Solidworks design and 3D printing. Car assembly and optimization, making sure it runs reliably with no stuttering and drives in straight line

Media

Video, photographs, social media. Publicity. Sponsorship opportunities.

Administration

Logistics (including budgets), project management, recruitment
