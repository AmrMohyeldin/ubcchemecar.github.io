Introduction

UBC Chem-E-Car is one of 22 undergraduate teams recognized as an Engineering Design Team (EDT) in the University of British Columbia.

We specialize in safely transforming potential energy stored in chemicals to mechanical energy as well as accurate control of chemical reactions for use as chemical timing.

The UBC Chem-E-Car team is one of the few EDTs that represent chemical engineers. We provide a platform for gaining hands-on experience in clean energy production. Many of our former team members have started exciting careers fuel cell and battery-related work

We compete in the annual American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE) Chem-E-Car competition.


Shoe-box sized car

The competition involves making a shoe-box sized car that travels a certain distance with a given cargo in under 2 minutes.

Stopping Mechanism

To complicate the otherwise trivial task, the car’s stopping mechanism must be chemical – no physical or electronic clocks and sensors can be used for stopping.

The Challenge

Furthermore, we are only given the cargo and travel distance 1 hour ahead of the competition. Extensive knowledge of the reactions involved are required for accurate control of the car.
