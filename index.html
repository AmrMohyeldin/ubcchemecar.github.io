Introduction

UBC Chem-E-Car is one of 22 undergraduate teams recognized as an Engineering Design Team in the University of British Columbia.

We specialize in safely transforming potential energy stored in chemicals to mechanical energy as well as accurate control of chemical reactions for use as chemical timing.

We compete in the annual American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE) Chem-E-Car competition.

Chem-E-Car Competition

Shoe-box sized car

The competition involves making a shoe-box sized car travel a certain distance with a given cargo in under 2 minutes.

Stopping Mechanism

To complicate the otherwise trivial task, the car’s stopping mechanism must be chemical – which means no physical or electronic clocks or sensors can be used for stopping.

The Challenge

We do not know the cargo or the distance to travel until 1 hour before competition. It is crucial to have adequate knowledge of the reactions involved to be able to calculate the required chemicals.

What We Do

Want to join us? We will not turn away any dedicated, hard working students that contribute to the project.

Chemical

Design of car stopping mechanism using the iodine clock reaction. Involves wet lab experiments

Battery

Research battery material and composition, design battery casing, perform circuitry integration

Electrical

Circuit board fabrication, circuitry layout design, soldering and assemblying PCB and Arduino programming

Mechanical

Solidworks design and 3D printing. Car assembly and optimization, making sure it runs reliably with no stuttering and drives in straight line
